Hello, my name is Lizbeth Vega. I am in my third year of undergraduate school at University of California, Riverside. I am currently studying english literature in hopes to be a highschool english teacher. I will be applying for graduate school in the fall for a teaching credential and masters. As of right now, I work at an elementary school and tutor students who are struggling in their studies. I have loved working with the students, but I do not see myself teaching multiple subjects a day. Ever since I was young, I have always wanted to be a teacher, I just never knew what subject or grade I wanted to teach. When I graduated high school, I attended community college and took a lot of classes, but I still had no idea what I wanted to teach. Finally, during my third year I took a world literature class and my life was changed forever. I knew what I wanted to major in and teach. After four years at a community college, I finally transferred to UCR. I currently hold a 4.0 GPA and I am expected to finish in less than two years. Going from having no sense of purpose or direction, to knowing exactly what I wanted to do took a long time. I am so thankful for my english professor at my community college, but I wish I had met her sooner. The reason I am pursuing the career of a high school english teacher is because I want to be an inspiration to students who have no idea what they are going to do after highschool. Those students who might feel lost or scared about life after high school. Those students who are going to community college and might be feeling embarrassed; I was in the same boat.
